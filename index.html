02 · What it is

YouTube Shopping in one page

A set of features that turn long-form videos, Shorts and livestreams into transactional surfaces. The catalog comes from retailers via Google Merchant Center and/or Shopify; content lives on YouTube; the transaction is completed on the retailer's site (or in-app via Shopify Payments in the US).

The 5 shopping surfaces

🏪
Channel Store
"Store" tab on the creator's page with the full catalog
🛍️
Product Shelf
Dynamic carousel below or beside the video player
▶️
Shopping Button
Interactive icon in the corner of the video or Short
🏷️
Product Stickers / Tags
Product image on screen at the timestamp where it's mentioned
📡
Pinned Live
Featured product in chat during a livestream
03 · How it works

Three operating models

CORE

Affiliate Program

For creators · 500+ subs · 12 eligible countries

The creator tags products from partnered retailers' catalogs (Nike, Sephora, Walmart, etc.) and earns commission on attributed sales.

  • Commission 5–20% (15% median)
  • Attribution window 30–50 days
  • Scalable volume bonuses
  • Paid via AdSense in 60–120 days

Direct Store

For creators with their own brand · 1,000+ subs

The creator connects their store (Shopify, Spring, Spreadshop, Fourthwall) and sells their own merchandise directly from videos and Channel Store.

  • Catalog auto-tagging
  • Automatic "YouTube Merch Shelf"
  • Embedded checkout in the US

BrandConnect / Creator Partnerships

For brands + creators · Unified hub Mar 2026

Marketplace inside Google Ads where brands find creators for sponsored content and Shopping campaigns. Dynamic brand deal slots in long-form videos (testing 2026).

  • AI-powered creator-brand fit suggestions
  • Reusable / re-sellable slots
  • Available in US, India, Indonesia, UK, Brazil, Australia, Canada

05 · Note for the board

For this report we don't have real video examples because:

! YouTube Shopping native features are not visible from our region

The product shelf, the "View products" button, Shorts stickers and in-app checkout are not universal. Their appearance depends on three factors that explain why the board won't see them by clicking on any video from their computer:

  • Geography: advanced tagging and the Affiliate Program are live primarily in the US, Korea, India, Brazil, Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, Malaysia, the Philippines and Singapore. From Uruguay, Argentina, Spain or Mexico the UI hides the native elements and only shows description links.
  • Device: the Shopping Button and stickers are optimized for the YouTube mobile app. On desktop (web) they turn into a Product Shelf below the player or simply don't appear.
  • Creator setup: each creator decides which products to show and at which specific timestamps the tags appear.

Workaround for this report: the three videos embedded below are official testimonials from the @YouTubeCreators channel where the creators themselves explain and show how they use the platform. They serve as verifiable evidence without depending on local visualization.

07 · Reference brands

Documented brand cases

Examples of brands operating with YouTube Shopping based on editorial coverage and published case studies. We can't visually verify the tags or the native Shopping experience from our region (see section 05) — the available evidence is the press article or video referenced in each card.

BK Beauty
DTC · Makeup brushes

The play: Lisa Howigi (creator of the Lisa J Makeup channel) launched her own brand of makeup brushes because brushes appear in EVERY makeup video. She sells her brushes while doing tutorials with other brands' products.

The result: USD 1M in year 1 → USD 10M+ by 2023. Anchor case in YouTube's official workSHOPPED podcast (Apr 2026).

→ PPC Land case study
PillowtalkDerm (Dr. Idriss)
DTC · Skincare · NYC

The play: dermatologist built a community of 3M "Nerds" over 6 years with educational videos before selling any product. Co-founder is an ex-L'Oréal marketing veteran. Launched with only 3 products focused on hyperpigmentation (the "Major Fade" system).

The result: sold out in less than 36 hours, entered Sephora USA, won an Allure Best of Beauty award. Official workSHOPPED YouTube episode (Mar 2026).

→ workSHOPPED · official episode
HexClad
DTC · Cookware

The play: premium pans (USD 200-400) are hard to sell online because people want to "see them". Solution: invade YouTube with cooking creators (Joshua Weissman, ChefSteps, etc.) demonstrating the product in real use + Gordon Ramsay as ambassador.

The result: USD 350M in revenue, scaling ~10× in 4 years leveraging YouTube as the primary demo channel.

→ Digital Chapter · growth deep dive
SACHEU Beauty
DTC · Beauty · Creator-turned-brand

The play: Sarah Cheung (creator with ~870K YT subs) launched her line with a single hero product solving a concrete problem: the Lip Liner STAY-N peel-off. The brand grows on the back of tagging videos and reviews on YouTube and TikTok.

The result: 4 billion product views on TikTok, bestseller ranking at Ulta and Sephora, Forbes 30 Under 30. Cited by name in YouTube's official TR25 Shopping Report as anchor case for "viral product via YT Shopping".

→ YouTube · TR25 Shopping Report
Glossier
Beauty · Historic case 2022

The play: first brand to sell via Shoppable Shorts. Launched the No.1 Pencil eyeliner with artist Kehlani and the hashtag #WrittenInGlossier — every Short with the hashtag automatically generated a shopping banner.

The result: foundational case for the Shoppable Shorts category on YouTube.

08 · Trafilea perspective

Actionable plays for the portfolio

Direct mapping of the learnings to Trafilea brands (Shapermint, Truekind, SWC, BodCon). DTC categories with high affinity: shapewear, intimates, beauty.

1

Apply as a Shopify brand merchant

Trafilea already runs on Shopify Plus. Enable the "Google & YouTube" app to connect the Shapermint/Truekind catalog to the affiliate program. Playbook: BK Beauty, HexClad and GoPure Beauty as direct DTC early-adopter references.

Impact: access to the long tail of women 35–65 lifestyle/fashion creators
2

Plug the catalog to 50–100 micro-creators

Identify and onboard nano/micro-creators (10K–100K subs) in lifestyle/fashion/beauty for women 30+. The BK Beauty insight is clear: 20K-sub creators convert better than million-sub stars thanks to deeper trust.

Impact: similar to the existing Shapermint Creators model, scaled to YouTube
3

Community-driven Shorts challenges

Launch a hashtag challenge in Shorts (e.g. #SmoothFitChallenge) with in-house creators + organic UGC, tagging products in vertical format. Combine with an open brief to the community to iterate the product.

Impact: organic virality + direct conversion
4

BF/CM 2026 with live shopping

Coordinate 1–2 live shopping events Holiday Stream and Shop-style with 1 anchor creator from the women 35-65 segment. Exclusive drops, discounts, urgency. The US live shopping market goes from USD 17B (2024) to USD 35B (2026E).

Impact: combine BF/CM Meta-driven with a complementary channel
⚠

Risks to monitor

Complex cross-channel attribution with Meta paid (cannibalization vs. complement). Federated ecosystem vs TikTok's native checkout. Creator compliance on shapewear claims (sizing, results). Clear rules to avoid cannibalization with the in-house BodCon network.

Mitigation: controlled Phase 1 pilot
